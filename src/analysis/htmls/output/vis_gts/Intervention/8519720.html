Double - blind randomized evaluation of intercostal_2 nerve_2 blocks_2 as an adjuvant to subarachnoid_2 administered_2 morphine_2 for post - thoracotomy analgesia .

BACKGROUND AND OBJECTIVES Thoracotomy is associated with pain and compromised pulmonary function . Intercostal_2 nerve_2 blocks_2 (_2 INB_2 )_2 and subarachnoid_2 morphine_2 (_2 SM_2 )_2 act on different portions of the pain pathway . Each is effective for post - thoracotomy pain relief . The combination of these two modalities in relieving post - thoracotomy pain and improving postoperative pulmonary function has not been investigated .
METHODS In a double - blind study , 20 patients undergoing lateral thoracotomy for lung resection were randomized to receive 0.5 mg SM_2 preoperatively_1 and_1 INB_2 with_1 bupivacaine_2 (_1 INB+_1 )_1 prior to wound closure or 0.5 mg SM_2 with_1 INB_2 using_1 saline_2 (_1 INB-_1 )_1 ._1 Visual analog scale pain scores at rest , with cough , and with movement of the ipsilateral arm , forced expiratory volume in 1 second ( FEV1 ) , and forced vital capacity ( FVC ) were measured at 4 , 24 , 48 , and 72 hours after the operation . Opioid use was measured during the initial 24 hours after the operation .
RESULTS At 4 hours , the INB+_1 group demonstrated better FEV1 ( 56.6% vs. 40.4% of baseline , P < .05 ) and FVC values ( 54.6% vs. 39.6% of baseline , P < .05 ) and less resting and cough pain ( P < .05 ) . However , FEV1 continued to decline in the INB+_1 group at 24 hours to lower than the INB-_1 group although pain scores were similar beyond 4 hours . Opioid usage during the first 24 hours was similar ( INB-_1 ,_1 16.7 mg vs. INB+_1 ,_1 13.2 mg , P = .7 ) .
CONCLUSIONS Although postoperative INB_2 provided modest improvements in pain and pulmonary function when used as an adjuvant to 0.5 mg SM_1 for post - thoracotomy analgesia , the benefits were transient .
