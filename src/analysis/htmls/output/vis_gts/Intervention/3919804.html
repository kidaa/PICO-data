Rapid tightening of blood glucose control leads to transient deterioration of retinopathy in insulin dependent diabetes mellitus : the Oslo study .

In a study of retinopathy during one year of tight blood glucose control 45 type I ( insulin dependent ) diabetics without proliferative retinopathy were randomised to receive either continuous_1 subcutaneous_1 insulin_2 infusion_1 ,_1 multiple_1 insulin_2 injections_1 ,_1 or conventional_1 insulin_2 treatment_1 (_1 controls_1 )_1 ._1 Near normoglycaemia was achieved with continuous_1 infusion_1 and multiple_1 injections_1 but not with conventional_1 treatment_1 ._1 Blind evaluation of fluorescein angiograms performed three monthly showed progression of retinopathy in the control_1 group , transient deterioration in the continuous_1 infusion_1 group , and no change in the multiple_1 injection_1 group . Half the patients receiving continuous_1 infusion_1 and multiple_1 injections_1 developed retinal cotton wool spots after three to six months . These changes regressed in all but four patients after 12 months . Control_1 patients did not develop cotton wool spots . Patients who developed cotton wool spots are characterised by a larger decrement in glycosylated haemoglobin and blood glucose values , more frequent episodes of hypoglycaemia , a longer duration of diabetes , and more severe retinopathy at onset . A large and rapid fall in blood glucose concentration may promote transient deterioration of diabetic retinopathy .
