Effect of rosuvastatin_2 monotherapy or in combination with fenofibrate_2 or ω-3_2 fatty_2 acids_2 on lipoprotein subfraction profile in patients with mixed dyslipidaemia and metabolic syndrome .

BACKGROUND Raised triglycerides ( TG ) , decreased high - density lipoprotein cholesterol ( HDL - C ) levels and a predominance of small dense low density lipoproteins ( sdLDL ) are characteristics of the metabolic syndrome ( MetS ) .
OBJECTIVE To compare the effect of high_1 -_1 dose_1 rosuvastatin_2 monotherapy_1 with moderate dosing combined with fenofibrate_2 or ω-3_2 fatty_2 acids_2 on the lipoprotein subfraction profile in patients with mixed dyslipidaemia and MetS.
METHODS We previously randomised patients with low - density lipoprotein cholesterol ( LDL - C ) > 160 and TG > 200   mg / dl to rosuvastatin_2 monotherapy_1 40   mg / day ( R_1 group , n = 30 ) or rosuvastatin_2 10_1 mg_1 /_1 day_1 combined_1 with_1 fenofibrate_2 200 mg / day ( RF_1 group , n = 30 ) or ω-3_2 fatty_2 acids_2 2 g / day ( Rω_1 group , n = 30 ) . In the present study , only patients with MetS were included ( 24 , 23 and 24 in the R_1 ,_1 RF_1 and Rω_1 groups respectively ) . At baseline and after 12 weeks of treatment , the lipoprotein subfraction profile was determined by polyacrylamide 3% gel electrophoresis .
RESULTS The mean LDL size was significantly increased in all groups . This change was more prominent with RF_1 than with other treatments in parallel with its greater hypotriglyceridemic capacity ( p < 0.05 compared with R_1 and Rω_1 )_1 . A decrease in insulin resistance by RF_1 was also noted . Only RF_1 significantly raised HDL - C levels ( by 7.7% , p < 0.05 ) by increasing the cholesterol of small HDL particles . The cholesterol of larger HDL subclasses was significantly increased by R_1 and Rω._1
CONCLUSIONS All regimens increased mean LDL size ; RF_1 was the most effective . A differential effect of treatments was noted on the HDL subfraction profile .
