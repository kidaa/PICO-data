The effects of sevoflurane_2 and isoflurane_2 on recovery from outpatient surgery .

This randomized , open - label study compared the investigational inhalational anesthetic sevoflurane_2 with isoflurane_2 in 47 healthy women undergoing elective ambulatory surgery . The women were randomized to receive either sevoflurane_2 or isoflurane_2 in 60% nitrous oxide - oxygen . Induction with thiopental 3 - 6 mg / kg was followed by vecuronium 0.1 mg / kg and fentanyl 0 - 200 micrograms . Duration of anesthesia , time to emergence , orientation , length of stay in the surgical unit , and hospital discharge were recorded . The emergence , length of stay , and discharge times after discontinuation of sevoflurane_2 were 9.7 + /- 0.7 , 120.6 + /- 8.0 , and 244 + /- 15 minutes , respectively , and for isoflurane_2 were 11.9 + /- 1.4 , 106.8 + /- 7.1 , and 282 + /- 24 minutes , respectively ( NS ) . The isoflurane_2 group had a higher frequency of postoperative cough . At the end of surgery , the sevoflurane_2 group received a deeper level of anesthesia ( minimum alveolar concentration 1.5 vs 1.3 ) , however , these patients were oriented earlier ( 13.6 + /- 1.1 min vs 17.0 + /- 1.5 min isoflurane_2 ;_2 p = 0.02 ) after discontinuation of anesthesia , although this difference is of little clinical significance .
