PURPOSE To evaluate the effect on intraocular pressure ( IOP ) of switching from timolol_2 to latanoprost_2 or adding latanoprost_2 to timolol_2 in patients with open angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension where IOP is not adequately controlled with timolol .
METHODS This was a 6-week , double - masked , randomised multi - centre study . 53 patients with primary open angle glaucoma , capsular glaucoma , or ocular hypertension with an IOP of at least 21 mmHg on current therapy were recruited . After a run - in period of at least 2 weeks on timolol_1 ,_1 5 mg / ml twice daily , patients were randomised to one of three groups . One group continued on timolol_2 ,_2 one switched from timolol_2 to latanoprost_2 ,_2 50 microg / ml once daily , and a third group received latanoprost_2 in addition to timolol_2 ._2 The efficacy was evaluated by comparing IOP at 9 AM at baseline and after 6 weeks of treatment .
RESULTS IOP at baseline and after 6 weeks of treatment ( mean + /- SEM ) were 24.2 + /- 0.9 and 23.8 + /- 1.0 mmHg ( n = 16 ) for patients continuing on timolol_2 ,_2 26.3 + /- 1.2 and 19.6 + /- 1.1 mmHg ( n = 17 ) for patients switching to latanoprost_2 ,_2 and 23.2 + /- 1.0 and 17.5 + /- 0.8 mmHg ( n = 17 ) for patients with combined_1 treatment_1 ._1 Adding latanoprost_2 to timolol_2 reduced IOP with 5.9 + /- 0.9 mmHg ( p < 0.001 ) and switching from timolol_2 to latanoprost_2 reduced IOP with 5.0 + /- 0.9 mmHg ( p < 0.001 ) , which caused in each group a significant IOP reduction of about 25% .
CONCLUSIONS The effect of latanoprost_2 was additive to that of timolol_1 ,_1 and a good effect on IOP reduction was also achieved by switching from timolol_2 to latanoprost_2 ,_2 suggesting that a switch in many patients is an effective alternative to combination_1 treatment_1 ._1
