[ The sympatho - adrenergic stress reaction in ear surgery using various anesthesia technics ] .

The aim of the present study was to investigate the effects of various anaesthetic procedures on the endocrine stress responses during ear microsurgical operations . Simple mastoidectomies , radical mastoidectomies and tympano plastics were carried out in 49 patients under the following randomised anaesthetic procedures : Group 1 halothane_2 anaesthesia_2 and_2 retroauricular_2 infiltration_2 anaesthesia_2 with_2 lidocaine_2 and_2 ornipressin_2 ( n = 14 ) , Group 2 fentanyl_2 anaesthesia_2 and_2 retroauricular_2 anaesthesia_2 with_2 lidocaine_2 and_2 ornipressin_2 ( n = 10 ) , Group 3 fentanyl_2 anaesthesia_2 and_2 retroauricular_2 infiltration_2 anaesthesia_2 with_2 lidocaine_2 and_2 epinephrine_2 ( n = 14 ) , and Group 4 retroauricular_2 infiltration_2 anaesthesia_2 with_2 prilocaine_2 and_2 epinephrine_2 ( n = 14 ) . The plasma levels of epinephrine , norepinephrine , glucose , lactate and free glycerol were measured in addition to mean arterial pressure ( MAP ) and heart rate ( HR ) immediately before anaesthesia , 10 minutes after skin incision , 10 minutes after having started bone drilling , at the end of the operation and 3 hours after operation . All data were subjected to covariance analysis including the age factor . Plasma catecholamine concentrations remained within the normal range during the investigation in patients subjected to general anaesthesia ( Groups 1 - 3 ) . Plasma catecholamines ( epinephrine and norepinephrine ) increased significantly in Group 4 ( retroauricular infiltration anaesthesia ) . There were no group variabilities with regard to MAP and HR . The plasma levels of epinephrine and norepinephrine demonstrate a direct response to stress followed by a secondary change in glucose , lactate and free glycerol .
