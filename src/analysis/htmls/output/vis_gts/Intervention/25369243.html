Atomoxetine_2 in autism spectrum disorder : no effects on social functioning ; some beneficial effects on stereotyped behaviors , inappropriate speech , and fear of change .

UNLABELLED Abstract Objective : The objective of this study was to investigate the short - term treatment effects of atomoxetine_2 on autism spectrum disorder ( ASD ) symptoms in children and adolescents with both ASD and attention - deficit / hyperactivity disorder ( ADHD ) .
METHODS A total of 97 patients 6 - 17 years of age , with ASD and ADHD , were treated with 1.2 â€‰ mg / kg / day of atomoxetine_2 during an 8 week double - blind placebo_2 -_2 controlled period . Here , we investigated effects on two parent - based secondary outcome measures , the Aberrant Behavior Checklist ( ABC ) and the Children 's Social Behavior Questionnaire ( CSBQ ) .
RESULTS After 8 weeks of double - blind treatment , atomoxetine_2 administration was associated with significant treatment effects on the ABC subscales Hyperactivity , Inappropriate Speech , and Stereotypic Behavior , and on the CSBQ subscale Fear for Changes .
CONCLUSIONS Our study results indicate no beneficial effects of atomoxetine_1 on social functioning . However , atomoxetine_2 may ameliorate restricted and stereotyped behaviors and communication . This study has been registered in ClinicalTrials.gov ( www.clinicaltrials.gov ) under registration number NCT00380692 .
