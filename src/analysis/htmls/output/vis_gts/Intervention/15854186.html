Cophenylcaine_2 spray_1 vs. placebo_2 in flexible nasendoscopy : a prospective double - blind randomised controlled trial .

Practices vary across the UK on the use of topical preparation prior to flexible fibreoptic nasendoscopy . In this double - blind study , we randomised 98 patients to receive cophenylcaine_2 or placebo_2 nasal_1 spray_1 before flexible nasendoscopy . A visual analogue scale ( 1 - 100 ) was used to record pain , unpleasantness of taste and overall discomfort experienced . Overall , the procedure was associated with minimal pain and discomfort in both groups . There was no significant difference in pain or overall discomfort experienced between the two groups ; however , the sensation of bad taste was significantly worse in the cophenylcaine_1 group . In linear regression , factors that predicted the overall unpleasantness of the experience were primarily pain experienced and secondarily unpleasantness of taste . We conclude that the routine use of cophenylcaine_2 for nasal preparation is not justified before flexible nasendoscopy .
