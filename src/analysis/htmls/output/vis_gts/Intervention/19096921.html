Trial design challenges when combining_1 medication_1 and_1 parent_1 training_1 in children with pervasive developmental disorders .

This paper presents the rationale for a 24-week , randomized trial designed to test whether risperidone_2 plus_2 structured_2 parent_2 training_2 would be superior to risperidone_2 only_1 on measures of noncompliance , irritability and adaptive functioning . In this model , medication reduces tantrums , aggression and self - injury ; parent training promotes improvement in noncompliance and adaptive functioning . Thus , medication_1 and_1 parent_1 training_1 target related , but separate , outcomes . At week 24 , the medication was gradually withdrawn to determine whether subjects in the combined treatment group could be managed on a lower dose or off medication without relapse . Both symptom reduction and functional improvement are important clinical treatment targets .
