A novel approach to the use of subgingival controlled - release chlorhexidine_1 delivery in chronic periodontitis : a randomized clinical trial .

BACKGROUND We aimed to analyze clinical , microbiologic , and serologic effects of chlorhexidine_2 (_2 CHX_2 )_2 chips_2 used as a subgingival controlled - release delivery device before and immediately after scaling and root planing ( SRP ) .
METHODS Twenty - four patients presenting with ≥12 teeth with probing depth ( PD ) ≥5 mm and bleeding on probing were assigned in test or control groups . After prophylaxis , CHX_1 chips_1 (_1 test_1 )_1 or placebo_2 chips_2 (_1 control_1 )_1 were placed in pockets with PD ≥5 mm . Ten days later , SRP was performed in all teeth with PD ≥4 mm in a single appointment . Immediately after SRP , new chips were inserted in all pockets with PD ≥5 mm . Parameters were assessed at baseline ; beginning of SRP ; and 1 , 3 , and 6 months after treatment . Subgingival samples were obtained at baseline ; beginning of SRP ; and at 1 month after treatment . Periodontal pathogens Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans , Porphyromonas gingivalis , Prevotella intermedia , Tannerella forsythia , and Treponema denticola were analyzed . Serum levels of high sensitive C - reactive and lipopolysaccharide - binding proteins were measured . The changes of the parameters between and within the groups were tested by Mann - Whitney U test ( P < 0.05 ) .
RESULTS All clinical and serologic parameters improved in both groups over time . There was a significant difference in clinical attachment level ( CAL ) gain from baseline to 6 months between groups ( 1.17 mm in the test group versus 0.79 mm in the placebo group ) ( P < 0.05 ) . The treatment with CHX_2 chips_1 showed a greater reduction of the microorganisms of the " red complex " after 1 month ( P = 0.02 ) .
CONCLUSION The use of CHX_2 chips_1 before and immediately after SRP improved CAL and reduced the subgingival microorganisms of the red complex in the treatment of chronic periodontitis .
