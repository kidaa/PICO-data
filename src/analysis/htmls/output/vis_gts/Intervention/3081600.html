Double - blind comparison of doxepin_2 versus bupropion_2 in outpatients with a major depressive disorder .

A double - blind controlled study comparing the effects of bupropion_2 to doxepin_2 in outpatients with primary depression was conducted to evaluate efficacy and safety differences between the two drugs . Following a 7-day placebo washout period , patients could be treated for up to 13 weeks on either treatment . Antidepressant response was assessed by the Hamilton Depression and Anxiety Scales , Clinical Global Severity and Improvement Ratings , and the Zung Self - Rating Depression Scale . Comparable efficacy between the compounds was found across the 13-week study . Doxepin_2 differed from bupropion_2 mainly on the sleep factor of the Hamilton Depression Scale , with doxepin_2 improving sleep to a greater extent than bupropion_2 ._2 Doxepin_2 produced a greater incidence of anticholinergic side effects , including dry mouth , constipation , sleepiness , and tiredness , in comparison to bupropion_2 ._2 Also , increased appetite and weight gain were consistent side effects of doxepin_2 relative to bupropion_2 ._2
