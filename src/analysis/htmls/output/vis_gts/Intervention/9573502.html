Systemic hemodynamic , neurohormonal , and renal effects of a steady - state infusion of human_2 brain_2 natriuretic_2 peptide_2 in patients with hemodynamically decompensated heart failure .

BACKGROUND Human_2 brain_2 natriuretic_2 peptide_2 (_2 hBNP_2 )_2 is a promising agent for the treatment of decompensated cardiac failure . However , the systemic hemodynamic , neurohormonal , and renal effects of hBNP_2 have been incompletely studied in human heart failure .
METHODS AND RESULTS The effects of a continuous 4-hour infusion of hBNP_2 were determined in 16 decompensated heart failure patients in an invasive , randomized , double - blind , placebo_2 -_2 controlled study . Patients were evaluated during three 4-hour study periods : baseline , treatment ( placebo_2 [ n = 4 ] versus hBNP_2 0.025 or 0.05 microgram / kg / min [ n = 12 ] ) , and post - treatment . Urinary volume losses were replaced hourly to separate the vasodilatory and diuretic effects of hBNP . Two patients in the hBNP_2 group were excluded from the analysis because of adverse events . hBNP_2 significantly ( P < .001 ) reduced right atrial pressure and pulmonary capillary wedge pressure by approximately 30% and 40% , respectively . hBNP_2 also significantly lowered systemic vascular resistance from 1722 + /- 139 to 1101 + /- 83 dynes.s.cm-5 ( P < .05 ) . These unloading effects of hBNP_2 produced a 28% increase in cardiac index ( P < .05 ) with no change in heart rate . Compared to placebo_1 ,_1 hBNP_2 decreased plasma norepinephrine and aldosterone . Renal hemodynamics were unaffected by hBNP_2 ;_2 however , most patients were resistant to its natriuretic effect .
CONCLUSIONS 1 ) The predominant hemodynamic effects of hBNP_2 were a decrease in cardiac preload and systemic vascular resistance . 2 ) hBNP_2 also improved cardiac output without increasing heart rate . 3 ) Plasma norepinephrine and aldosterone levels decreased during hBNP_2 infusion . 4 ) hBNP_2 is pharmacologically active and has potential in the therapy for decompensated heart failure .
