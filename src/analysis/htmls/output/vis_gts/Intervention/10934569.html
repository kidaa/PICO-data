Randomized trial of intensive_2 early_2 intervention_2 for children with pervasive developmental disorder .

Young children with pervasive developmental disorder were randomly assigned to intensive_2 treatment_2 or parent_2 training_2 ._2 The intensive_2 treatment_2 group ( 7 with autism , 8 with pervasive developmental disorder not otherwise specified -- NOS ) averaged 24.52 hours per week of individual treatment for one year , gradually reducing hours over the next 1 to 2 years . The parent_2 training_2 group_1 ( 7 with autism , 6 with pervasive developmental disorder NOS ) received 3 to 9 months of parent training . The groups appeared similar at intake on all measures ; however , at follow - up the intensive_1 treatment_1 group outperformed the parent_1 training_1 group on measures of intelligence , visual - spatial skills , language , and academics , though not adaptive functioning or behavior problems . Children with pervasive developmental disorder NOS may have gained more than those with autism .
