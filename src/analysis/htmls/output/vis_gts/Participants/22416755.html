Preschoolers_2 acquire general knowledge by sharing in pretense .

Children_2 acquire general knowledge about many kinds of things , but there are few known means by which this knowledge is acquired . In this article , it is proposed that children_1 acquire generic knowledge by sharing in pretend play . In Experiment 1 , twenty_2 -_2 two_2 3-_2 to_2 4-year_2 -_2 olds_2 watched pretense in which a puppet represented a " nerp " ( an unfamiliar kind of animal ) . For instance , in one scenario , the nerp ate and disliked a carrot . When subsequently asked generic questions about real nerps , children 's responses suggested that they had learned general facts ( e.g. , nerps dislike carrots ) . In Experiment 2 , thirty_2 -_2 two_2 4-_2 to_2 5-year_2 -_2 olds_2 learned from scenarios lacking pretend speech or sound effects . The findings reveal a long overlooked means by which children_2 can acquire generic knowledge .
