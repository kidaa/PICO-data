[ Intravenous treatment of postpartum_2 anemia_2 with trivalent ferrum preparation ] .

AIM OF THE STUDY To assess the effectivity and safeness of intravenous treatment of pospartal_2 anemia_2 with trivalent ferrum preparation .
TYPE OF THE STUDY Prospective randomized study .
SETTING Department_1 of_1 Obstetric_1 and_1 Gynecology_1 2nd_1 Medical_1 Faculty_1 Charles_1 University_1 and_1 Teaching_1 Hospital_1 Motol_1 ,_1 Prague_1 ,_1 and_1 Department_1 of_1 Obstetric_1 and_1 Gynecology_1 1st_1 Medical_1 Faculty_1 and_1 Teaching_1 Hospital_1 Bulovka_1 ,_1 Prague_1 ._1
METHODS 500 mg of sacharose ferric oxide ( Venofer ) was intravenously administered in two days regimen to 50_2 women_2 with_2 clinical_2 and_2 lab_2 signs_2 of_2 postpartal_2 anemia_2 ._2 The effect of administered drug was determined by comparsion of values of red blood count recovered before the treatment , 2nd or 3rd day post administration and two weeks later . The serum values of soluble transferrin receptors and ferritin were observed as markers of iron cell saturation and body iron reserves , too . Integral part of the study was the monitoring of adverse events during the treatment .
RESULTS Venofer came in sight as effective drug in the treatment of pospartal_1 anemia_1 and could become as the alternative to blood transfusion in mid - severe cases . It should be emphasized that we have not encountered any serious adverse event with intravenous trivalent saccharose ferric oxide treatment .
