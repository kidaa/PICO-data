Prediction of creatinine_1 clearance_1 from serum creatinine in patients with rheumatoid arthritis : comparison of six formulae and one nomogram .

The estimation of glomerular filtration rate is important for the medical treatment of patients with rheumatoid arthritis ( RA ) . However , the determination of endogenous_1 creatinine_1 clearance_1 (_1 Clcr_1 )_1 from a 24-h urine collection is an unreliable and time - consuming procedure . We therefore tested the accuracy of six equations and one nomogram for the prediction of Clcr_1 from serum creatinine ( Scr ) in 38 patients with RA and 20 controls . A positive correlation was found for all methods in the controls ( r = 0.83 - 0.94 ) and RA patients ( r = 0.51 - 0.69 ) . The methods did not overestimate Clcr_1 in RA . In the RA group the simple formula published by Cockcroft [ Clcr = ( ( 140 - age ) x body weight)/(72 x Scr ) , x 0.85 for females ] showed the best correlation with the measured Clcr_1 ._1 In RA the Cockroft formula can reliably be used to predict Clcr_1 from Scr .
