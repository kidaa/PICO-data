Effect of rosuvastatin monotherapy or in combination with fenofibrate or ω-3 fatty acids on lipoprotein_1 subfraction_1 profile_1 in patients with mixed dyslipidaemia and metabolic syndrome .

BACKGROUND Raised triglycerides ( TG ) , decreased high - density lipoprotein cholesterol ( HDL - C ) levels and a predominance of small dense low density lipoproteins ( sdLDL ) are characteristics of the metabolic syndrome ( MetS ) .
OBJECTIVE To compare the effect of high - dose rosuvastatin monotherapy with moderate dosing combined with fenofibrate or ω-3 fatty acids on the lipoprotein_1 subfraction_1 profile_1 in patients with mixed dyslipidaemia and MetS.
METHODS We previously randomised patients with low - density lipoprotein cholesterol ( LDL - C ) > 160 and TG > 200   mg / dl to rosuvastatin monotherapy 40   mg / day ( R group , n = 30 ) or rosuvastatin 10 mg / day combined with fenofibrate 200 mg / day ( RF group , n = 30 ) or ω-3 fatty acids 2 g / day ( Rω group , n = 30 ) . In the present study , only patients with MetS were included ( 24 , 23 and 24 in the R , RF and Rω groups respectively ) . At baseline and after 12 weeks of treatment , the lipoprotein_1 subfraction_1 profile_1 was determined by polyacrylamide 3% gel electrophoresis .
RESULTS The mean_1 LDL_1 size_1 was significantly increased in all groups . This change was more prominent with RF than with other treatments in parallel with its greater hypotriglyceridemic_1 capacity_1 ( p < 0.05 compared with R and Rω ) . A decrease in insulin_1 resistance_1 by RF was also noted . Only RF significantly raised HDL_1 -_1 C_1 levels_1 ( by 7.7% , p < 0.05 ) by increasing the cholesterol of small HDL particles . The cholesterol_1 of_1 larger_1 HDL_1 subclasses_1 was significantly increased by R and Rω.
CONCLUSIONS All regimens increased mean LDL_1 size_1 ;_1 RF was the most effective . A differential effect of treatments was noted on the HDL subfraction profile .
