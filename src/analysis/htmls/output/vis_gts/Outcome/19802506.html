The effect of weight training on bone_1 mineral_1 density_1 and bone_1 turnover_1 in postmenopausal breast cancer survivors with bone loss : a 24-month randomized controlled trial .

SUMMARY This study examined whether 24 months of weight training exercises enhanced the effectiveness of risedronate , calcium , and vitamin D in maintaining or improving bone_1 mineral_1 density_1 (_1 BMD_1 )_1 in 223 postmenopausal breast cancer survivors . Subjects who were > or = 50% adherent to exercise had no improvement in BMD_1 but were less likely to lose BMD_1 ._1
INTRODUCTION This study examined whether ( 1 ) postmenopausal breast cancer survivors ( BCS ) with bone loss taking 24 months of risedronate , calcium , and vitamin D had increased bone_1 mineral_1 density_1 (_1 BMD_1 )_1 at the total hip , femoral neck , L1-L4 spine , total radius and 33% radius , and decreased bone_1 turnover_1 ;_1 ( 2 ) subjects who also participated in strength / weight training ( ST ) exercises had greater increases in BMD_1 and greater decreases in bone_1 turnover_1 ;_1 and ( 3 ) subjects who also exercised were more likely to preserve ( at least maintain ) BMD_1 ._1
METHODS Postmenopausal BCS ( 223 ) were randomly assigned to exercise plus medication or medication only groups . Both groups received 24 months of 1,200 mg of calcium and 400 IU of vitamin D daily and 35 mg of risedronate weekly , and the exercise group additionally had ST exercises twice weekly .
RESULTS After 24 months , women who took medications without exercising had significant improvements in BMD_1 at the total hip ( + 1.81% ) and spine ( + 2.85% ) and significant decreases in Alkphase_1 B_1 ( -8.7% ) and serum_1 NTx_1 ( -16.7% ) . Women who also exercised had additional increases in BMD_1 at_1 the_1 femoral_1 neck_1 ( + 0.29% ) , total_1 hip_1 ( + 0.34% ) , spine_1 ( + 0.23% ) , total_1 radius_1 ( + 0.30% ) , and additional decreases in Alkphase_1 B_1 ( -2.4% ) and Serum_1 NTx_1 ( -6.5% ) . Additional changes in BMD_1 and bone_1 turnover_1 with exercise were not significant . Subjects who were > or = 50% adherent to exercise were less likely to lose BMD_1 at_1 the_1 total_1 hip_1 ( chi - square [ 1 ] = 4.66 , p = 0.03 ) and femoral_1 neck_1 ( chi - square [ 1 ] = 4.63 , p = 0.03 ) .
CONCLUSION Strength / weight training exercises may prevent loss of BMD_1 in postmenopausal BCS at risk for bone loss .
