Safety , tolerability , and immunogenicity after 1 and 2 doses of zoster_6 vaccine_6 in healthy adults ≥60 years of age .

BACKGROUND Incidence and severity of herpes zoster ( HZ ) and postherpetic neuralgia increase with age , associated with age - related decrease in immunity to varicella - zoster virus ( VZV ) . One dose of zoster_4 vaccine_4 (_4 ZV_4 )_4 has demonstrated substantial protection against HZ ; this study examined impact of a second dose of ZV_1 ._1
METHODS Randomized , double - blind , multicenter study with 210 subjects ≥60 years old compared immunity and safety profiles after one and two doses of ZV_3 ,_3 separated by 6 weeks , vs._1 placebo_5 ._5 Immunogenicity was evaluated using VZV interferon - gamma ( IFN-γ ) enzyme - linked immunospot ( ELISPOT ) assay and VZV glycoprotein enzyme - linked immunosorbent antibody ( gpELISA ) assay . Adverse experiences ( AEs ) were recorded on a standardized Vaccination Report Card .
RESULTS No serious vaccine - related AEs occurred . VZV IFN-γ ELISPOT geometric mean count ( GMC ) of spot - forming cells per 10(6 ) peripheral blood mononuclear cells increased in the ZV_1 group from 16.9 prevaccination to 49.5 and 32.8 at 2 and 6 weeks postdose 1 , respectively . Two weeks , 6 weeks and 6 months postdose 2 , GMC was 44.3 , 42.9 , and 36.5 , respectively . GMC in the placebo_3 group did not change during the study . The peak ELISPOT response occurred ∼2 weeks after each ZV_2 dose . The gpELISA geometric mean titers ( GMTs ) in the ZV_2 group were higher than in the placebo_2 group at 6 weeks after each dose . Correlation between the IFN-γ ELISPOT and gpELISA assays was poor .
CONCLUSIONS ZV_2 was generally well - tolerated and immunogenic in adults ≥60 years old . A second dose of ZV_2 was generally safe , but did not boost VZV_1 -_1 specific immunity beyond levels achieved postdose 1 .
