Effects of Saccharomyces_4 boulardii_4 in children with acute diarrhoea .

AIM Certain probiotic agents , e.g. Lactobacillus GG , have shown efficacy in clinical trials for the treatment of acute childhood diarrhoea , but few studies have examined the effect of Saccharomyces_2 boulardii_2 ._2 We evaluated the effect of S._3 boulardii_3 in children with acute diarrhoea .
METHODS Two hundred children were randomized to receive S._4 boulardii_4 in a granulated form in a daily dose of 250 mg ( S._2 boulardii_2 group ) or placebo_4 (_1 placebo_2 group ) for 5 d. Clinical and demographic characteristics on admission were similar between the study groups .
RESULTS The medians of the average stool frequency after the second day of the treatment were significantly lower in the S._1 boulardii_1 group than in the placebo_1 group ( p = 0.003 ) . The duration of diarrhoea significantly reduced in the S._2 boulardii_2 group compared with the placebo_1 group ( 4.7 vs 5.5 d , p = 0.03 ) . The effect of S._1 boulardii_1 on watery diarrhoea became apparent after the second day of the treatment . The duration of hospital stay was shorter in the S._2 boulardii_2 group than in the placebo_2 group ( 2.9 vs 3.9 d , p < 0.001 ) . Four children from the placebo_1 group versus only one child from the S._2 boulardii_2 group had persisting diarrhoea .
CONCLUSION The placebo_2 -_2 controlled study suggested that S._1 boulardii_1 significantly reduced the duration of acute diarrhoea and the duration of hospital stay . S._2 boulardii_2 seems to be a promising agent for the amelioration of the course of acute diarrhoea in children when used therapeutically .
