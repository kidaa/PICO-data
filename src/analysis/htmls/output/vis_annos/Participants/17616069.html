[ Intravenous treatment of postpartum_2 anemia_1 with trivalent ferrum preparation ] .

AIM OF THE STUDY To assess the effectivity and safeness of intravenous treatment of pospartal_1 anemia_1 with trivalent ferrum preparation .
TYPE OF THE STUDY Prospective randomized study .
SETTING Department_3 of_3 Obstetric_3 and_3 Gynecology_3 2nd_3 Medical_3 Faculty_3 Charles_3 University_3 and_3 Teaching_3 Hospital_3 Motol_3 ,_3 Prague_3 ,_3 and_3 Department_3 of_3 Obstetric_3 and_3 Gynecology_3 1st_3 Medical_3 Faculty_3 and_3 Teaching_3 Hospital_3 Bulovka_3 ,_3 Prague_3 ._3
_2 METHODS 500 mg of sacharose ferric oxide ( Venofer ) was intravenously administered in two days regimen to 50_7 women_7 with_7 clinical_7 and_7 lab_7 signs_7 of_7 postpartal_7 anemia_7 ._7 The effect of administered drug was determined by comparsion of values of red blood count recovered before the treatment , 2nd or 3rd day post administration and two weeks later . The serum values of soluble transferrin receptors and ferritin were observed as markers of iron cell saturation and body iron reserves , too . Integral part of the study was the monitoring of adverse events during the treatment .
RESULTS Venofer came in sight as effective drug in the treatment of pospartal anemia and could become as the alternative to blood transfusion in mid - severe cases . It should be emphasized that we have not encountered any serious adverse event with intravenous trivalent saccharose ferric oxide treatment .
