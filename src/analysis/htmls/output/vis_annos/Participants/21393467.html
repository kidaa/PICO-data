Humans spontaneously select a step frequency that minimizes the energy expenditure of walking . This selection might be embedded within the neural circuits that generate gait so that the optimum is pre - programmed for a given walking speed . Or perhaps step frequency is directly optimized , based on sensed feedback of energy expenditure . Direct optimization is expected to be slow due to the compounded effect of delays and iteration , whereas a pre - programmed mechanism presumably allows for faster step frequency selection , albeit dependent on prior experience .
